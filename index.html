Behavioral Public Economics

This site provides resources for the teaching of Behavioral Public Economics at the graduate level. It is an extension of the Graduate Student Behavioral Public Economics Boot Camp, held approximately every two years since 2022, and organized by Douglas Bernheim, Dmitry Taubinsky, Hunt Allcott, and Ben Lockwood. This site provides access to materials, lectures, and videos from those boot camps, as well as a self-contained collection of teaching and study materials that can be used in a variety of settings.

  • Boot Camp 2025 — A record of the in-person PhD boot camp held at Stanford University (August 21–23, 2025), including the agenda, full video lectures, speaker information, and more.
  • Mini-Course — A self-contained collection of teaching and learning materials for graduate-level instruction in Behavioral Public Economics. Ideal for self-study or integration into an economics curriculum.
