About the Boot Camp

The Behavioral Public Economics Boot Camp is a PhD-level event designed to equip graduate students with a deep understanding of behavioral approaches to public economics. It draws connections across subfields like health, labor, household finance, development, and energy.

The 2025 Boot Camp will be held at Stanford University, August 21–23, 2025, featuring lectures and discussions led by Doug Bernheim, Ben Lockwood, Dmitry Taubinsky, and invited scholars.

About the Mini-Course

The mini-course in Behavioral Public Economics is a remote-learning resource for those interested in the subject. It includes a brief overview of the topic(s), recorded lectures, problem sets, native source files, and a reading list. These resources come from previous iterations of the boot camp and are designed to be accessible to a wide audience.
